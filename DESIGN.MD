I have a 3D render/image that is the centerpiece. Build a hero section around it — the image fills the entire viewport, the layout and typography exist on top of it without competing with it.
The feel to capture:
Think editorial magazine meets film title card. Text is treated as a graphic element, not just information. The layout is intentionally asymmetric and tension-filled — elements are placed where they create visual weight, not where convention says they should go. Nothing is centered. Nothing is safe.
Typography approach:
Use two contrasting fonts — one heavy, wide or condensed display face for the title (high impact, geometric, the kind used in fashion editorials or sports branding), and one clean lightweight sans-serif for everything else. The title should feel oversized to the point of being almost aggressive — letters are large enough to be shapes, not just words. Tight line-height, tight or negative letter-spacing. The supporting text is the opposite: small, airy, restrained. The contrast between these two extremes is the whole point.
Layout approach:
Anchor navigation to the top, content to the bottom. The bottom of the viewport is where everything lives — split it loosely into a left side and a right side that don't mirror each other. One side gets the big typographic statement, the other gets small supporting copy. They don't need to sit at the same vertical position. Scatter a few tiny all-caps metadata labels (category, index number, location, etc.) as editorial texture. One thin horizontal rule separates the text zone from the metadata zone. Outer padding should be tight — text can approach or touch the screen edge.
What to preserve from my image:
Don't put anything heavy over the focal point of the render. The image should breathe. Use the typography to frame and complement the composition, not fill empty space for its own sake.
What to never do:
No centered layouts. No equal columns. No cards or containers around text. No safe, conventional hero patterns. The layout should feel like it was designed specifically for this image, not dropped onto it.
